# Terms of Service — Anti-Surveillance Clauses (DRAFT)

> This draft covers only the privacy/anti-surveillance sections of a full ToS.
> Standard sections (liability, disputes, IP, termination mechanics, etc.) are

## §X. Prohibited Uses — Monitoring and Surveillance

X.1 **No surveillance use.** The Service exists to help veterans pursue their
own benefits claims and to let Veterans Service Organizations ("Organizations")
assist veterans who have chosen to work with them. You may not use the Service,
or any data obtained through it, to monitor, track, profile, or surveil any
veteran or other individual, except as strictly necessary to provide casework
services the veteran has requested.

X.2 **No third-party monitoring.** You may not access or use the Service by or
on behalf of, or provide data obtained from the Service to, any of the
following for any purpose relating to an individual veteran: employers or
prospective employers; insurance carriers or underwriters; landlords; lenders
or credit bureaus; private investigators; data brokers; or any law-enforcement
or government agency except in response to valid legal process (see §X.6).

X.3 **No re-disclosure or resale.** Organizations may not sell, license, rent,
or otherwise disclose veteran data obtained through the Service to any third
party. Veteran data may be used solely for casework on behalf of the veteran
to whom it pertains.

X.4 **No aggregation for profiling.** You may not combine data obtained from
the Service with outside data sources to build profiles of veterans, infer
conditions or diagnoses they have not disclosed to you, or score or rank
veterans for any purpose other than casework triage within the Service.

X.5 **Honoring revocation.** When a veteran revokes sharing of a document or
record, the Organization's right to use that record ends immediately. Copies
made outside the Service (downloads, screenshots, transcriptions) must be
destroyed within [7] days of revocation, except where retention is required by
law or by the Organization's accreditation obligations, in which case the
Organization must notify the veteran of the retention basis.

X.6 **Legal process.** If you receive a subpoena, court order, or other legal
demand for veteran data obtained through the Service, you must (a) notify
[Benefits Navigator] promptly unless legally prohibited, and (b) where lawful,
notify the affected veteran before disclosure.

X.7 **Enforcement.** Violation of this section is a material breach. We may
suspend or terminate access immediately, notify affected veterans, and — where
the violation involves an accredited representative — report the conduct to
the VA Office of General Counsel. These remedies are in addition to any other
remedy at law or equity.

## §Y. Organization Obligations

Y.1 **Least privilege.** Organizations must limit staff access to veteran data
to staff with a casework need. Organizations with more than [10] staff members
must enable the Service's restricted-visibility mode or maintain an equivalent
documented access policy.

Y.2 **Staff security.** Organization staff accounts must use two-factor
authentication when the Service requires it. Credential sharing between staff
members is prohibited.

Y.3 **Departing staff.** Organizations must deactivate memberships of departed
staff within [3] business days.

Y.4 **Audit cooperation.** Organizations acknowledge that all access to
veteran records through the Service is logged and that veterans can see those
logs, including the accessing staff member's name and organization.

## §Z. Veteran Rights (restated in Terms)

The Service provides, and we commit to maintaining: (a) veteran-initiated
sharing with per-document control; (b) immediate revocation of sharing;
(c) a complete log, visible to the veteran, of access to their records by
others, including our own staff; (d) export and deletion of their data.
